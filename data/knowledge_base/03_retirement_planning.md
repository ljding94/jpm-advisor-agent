# Retirement Planning Principles

Retirement planning answers two questions: how much you need, and how to draw it
down without running out.

## How much you need

The classic shorthand is the **4% rule** (Bengen 1994): a portfolio of stocks and
bonds, withdrawn at 4% of its initial value (adjusted for inflation each year), has
historically lasted at least 30 years in nearly all rolling US periods. Equivalently,
you need ~25× your annual spending. Recent research (Morningstar 2023) suggests 3.7%
is a safer starting rate given current valuations and lower bond yields.

## Sequence-of-returns risk

The order of returns matters more than their average for someone drawing down. A
bad first decade in retirement can permanently impair the portfolio even if the
average return is fine. Mitigations:
- Hold 1–2 years of spending in cash/short bonds (a "cash bucket").
- Reduce equity exposure in the 5 years before and after retirement (the "retirement
  red zone").
- Use a dynamic withdrawal strategy that cuts spending after bad years.

## Social Security and pensions

For most US retirees, Social Security covers 30–40% of pre-retirement income.
Delaying claiming from age 62 to 70 increases the monthly benefit by ~76%. For
healthy retirees with longevity in the family, delaying is usually the highest
risk-free real return available.

## Healthcare

Fidelity's 2024 estimate: a 65-year-old couple retiring today will spend ~$315,000
on healthcare in retirement, *not* including long-term care. Plan accordingly with
HSAs (if eligible) and Medicare supplemental coverage.
